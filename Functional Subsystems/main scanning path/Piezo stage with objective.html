Piezo stage with objective

The objective lens is the final optical element in the two-photon microscope's scanning module. It is positioned so its back focal plane (BFP) coincides with the pivot point of the scan-tube lens system. This placement creates a telecentric scanning configuration, ensuring constant angle of incidence and focus across, and thus image quality, across the field of view.

Vertical (z) scanning in this microscope is achieved by a piezo stage that moves the objective lens. Although this configuration compromises perfect telecentricity, the resulting aberrations are negligible within the piezo stage's 0.5 mm range.

The piezo stage, nPoint nPFocus400 piezo stage, equipped with an M25 adapter compatible with the Olympus XLUMPLFLN20XW objective (purchased with the stage), requires a custom adapter to interface with the Thorlabs C4W cage cube. This cage cube, part of the PMT detection module, is positioned between the objective and the tube lens. The custom adapter can be fabricated from either plastic (100% fill) or metal. While debugging noise in the piezo positioning, we replaced the initial plastic adapter with a metal one. This replacement, though ultimately not addressing the noise issue, may have provided some vibration dampening. See details on the piezo stage for the 3D model and manufacturing of the custom adapter.
